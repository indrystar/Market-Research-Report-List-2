<p><p>The vehicle oxygen sensor market consists of two main types: Titanium Oxide Type and Zirconia Type. Titanium Oxide sensors use a titanium-based material to detect oxygen levels in the exhaust gas, while Zirconia sensors utilize zirconium dioxide for the same purpose. Both types are essential components in modern automotive emissions control systems, providing real-time feedback to the engine's control unit to optimize fuel economy and reduce harmful emissions. The choice between Titanium Oxide and Zirconia sensors depends on factors such as cost, durability, and compatibility with specific vehicle models.</p></p>
