<p><ul><li>Saccharin or Bitrex Test</li><li>Isoamyl Acetate Test</li><li>Irritant Smoke Test</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Qualitative respirator fit testing involves three main methods: Saccharin or Bitrex Test, Isoamyl Acetate Test, and Irritant Smoke Test. The Saccharin or Bitrex Test uses a sweet or bitter taste to assess the respirator seal, while the Isoamyl Acetate Test involves detecting a banana-like odor. The Irritant Smoke Test creates a visible irritant smoke to check for leaks in the respirator seal. These methods are commonly used to ensure proper fit and effectiveness of respirators in various industries.</p></p>
