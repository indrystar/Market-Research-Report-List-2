<p><p>Exosome diagnostics and therapeutics involve the isolation and analysis of exosomes, which are small extracellular vesicles that play a crucial role in intercellular communication. These exosomes contain various biomolecules, including proteins, nucleic acids, and lipids, which can provide valuable insights into the physiological and pathological processes within the body. Exosome-based diagnostics can be used for early detection, prognosis, and monitoring of various diseases, including cancer, infectious diseases, and neurological disorders. On the other hand, exosome therapeutics involve the use of exosomes as drug delivery vehicles or as therapeutic agents themselves.</p><p>The exosome diagnostic and therapeutic market is witnessing rapid growth due to the increasing demand for non-invasive diagnostic tools, the rising prevalence of chronic diseases, and advancements in exosome isolation and analysis technologies. Additionally, the growing interest in precision medicine and personalized healthcare is driving the adoption of exosome-based diagnostics and therapeutics.
