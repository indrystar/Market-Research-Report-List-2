<p><p>Straight catheters are commonly used in peritoneal dialysis procedures where the catheter is inserted directly into the peritoneal cavity. They are straight in design and allow for easy placement and removal. On the other hand, pigtail-curled catheters have a coiled or pigtail shape at the tip, which helps prevent migration. They are often preferred for long-term dialysis treatment due to their stability and reduced risk of complications. Both types of catheters play a crucial role in providing effective and efficient peritoneal dialysis treatment to patients.</p></p>
