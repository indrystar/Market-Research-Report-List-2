<p><p>Cellulose ether and its derivatives are widely used in various industries due to their unique properties. Methyl Cellulose (MC) is known for its excellent water retention abilities. Hydroxypropyl Methylcellulose (HPMC) is commonly used as a thickening agent in pharmaceuticals and construction materials. Hydroxymethyl Methylcellulose (HMC) is valued for its adhesive properties. Carboxymethyl Cellulose (CMC) is used as a stabilizer and thickener in food products. Other derivatives include ethyl cellulose, propyl cellulose, and cellulose acetate, each with their own specific applications in industries like pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics.</p></p>
