<p><p>Dental imaging systems are classified into two main types: intra-oral and extra-oral imaging systems. Intra-oral imaging systems are designed to capture detailed images of the teeth and surrounding structures within the mouth, providing a close-up view for diagnostic purposes. On the other hand, extra-oral imaging systems capture images of the entire head and neck region, offering a broader view for comprehensive analysis. Both types of imaging systems play a crucial role in modern dentistry by aiding in diagnosis, treatment planning, and monitoring of oral health conditions.</p></p>
