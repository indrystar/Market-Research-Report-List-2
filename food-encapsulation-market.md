<p><p>Food encapsulation is a process of enclosing food ingredients in a protective shell to improve stability, preservation, and targeted delivery. There are different types of food encapsulation markets, including microencapsulation, which involves particles ranging from 1-1000 µm in size, nanoencapsulation, which uses particles less than 100 nm in size, and hybrid encapsulation, a combination of both techniques. Microencapsulation is suitable for larger compounds, while nanoencapsulation is ideal for delivering bioactive compounds with enhanced bioavailability and targeted release. Hybrid encapsulation offers a versatile approach by combining the advantages of both methods.</p></p>
