<p><p>Condensing units are essential components of refrigeration systems that help remove heat from the system to keep it running efficiently. There are three main types of condensing units: Air-Cooled, Water-Cooled, and Evaporative. Air-Cooled condensing units use air to remove heat, making them suitable for small to medium-sized refrigeration systems. Water-Cooled units use water as a cooling medium and are often used in larger industrial applications. Evaporative condensing units use water and air to cool the refrigerant, making them energy-efficient options for certain environments.</p></p>
