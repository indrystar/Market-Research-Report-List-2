<p><ul><li>Non-targeted contrast-enhanced ultrasound</li><li>Targeted contrast-enhanced ultrasound</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Contrast-enhanced ultrasound is a medical imaging technique that utilizes ultrasound contrast agents to improve visualization of blood vessels and tissues. Non-targeted contrast-enhanced ultrasound involves the use of generic contrast agents to enhance overall blood flow and tissue perfusion. On the other hand, targeted contrast-enhanced ultrasound specifically targets certain molecules or receptors in order to provide more detailed and specific information about a particular area of interest. Both types of contrast-enhanced ultrasound have their own unique applications and benefits in clinical practice.</p></p>
