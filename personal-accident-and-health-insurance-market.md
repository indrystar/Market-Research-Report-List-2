<p><p>Personal Accident and Health Insurance markets can be classified into two types - Type I and Type II. In Type I market, insurers set standardized premiums for all policyholders based on their age, gender, and other demographic factors. Meanwhile, in Type II market, insurers customize premiums based on individual risk factors such as occupation, lifestyle, and health history. Type I market caters to a broad population with similar risk profiles, while Type II market provides tailored coverage for individuals with unique risk profiles.</p></p>
