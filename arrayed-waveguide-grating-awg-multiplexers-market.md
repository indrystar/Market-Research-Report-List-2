<p><p>Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) multiplexers are commonly classified into two types based on their thermal properties: Thermal AWGs and Athermal AWGs. Thermal AWGs require active temperature stabilization to maintain their peak performance, making them suitable for applications where precise temperature control is possible. On the other hand, Athermal AWGs do not require active temperature control, making them more versatile and easier to integrate into different systems. Both types of AWGs have distinct advantages and are used in various optical communication and networking applications.</p></p>
